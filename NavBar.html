Real Madrid FC

Real Madrid Club de Fútbol stands as the most successful and prestigious institution in the history of association football. Founded in 1902, the club has cultivated a legacy of unparalleled excellence, characterized by an unrelenting pursuit of victory and a "never say die" spirit that defines its identity.

Stadium

Bernabéu (Spanish: El Bernabéu) is a retractable roof football stadium in Madrid, Spain. With a seating capacity of 83,186 following its extensive renovation completed in late 2024, the stadium has the second-largest seating capacity for a football stadium in Spain. It has been the home stadium of Real Madrid since its completion in 1947.

Achievements

Real Madrid's legacy is defined by an unparalleled collection of silverware that cements its status as the most successful club in football history. Dominating the international stage, the club holds a record 15 UEFA Champions League titles, a feat highlighted by their historic "three-peat" between 2016 and 2018. This continental success is bolstered by 5 FIFA Club World Cups and 6 UEFA Super Cups, making them the most decorated team in global competitions. Domestically, the "Merengues" have maintained a firm grip on Spanish football with a record 36 La Liga titles and 20 Copa del Rey trophies. This sustained excellence across more than a century earned them the prestigious title of FIFA Club of the Century, a distinction that remains the ultimate testament to their sporting merit and global influence.

Head coach

Álvaro Arbeloa Coca (Spanish pronunciation: [ˈalβaɾo aɾβeˈloa ˈkoka]; born 17 January 1983) is a Spanish former professional footballer, currently the head coach of La Liga club Real Madrid. He predominantly played as a right-back, and occasionally on the left side.
